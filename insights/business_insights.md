# Business Insights

This document summarizes the key business findings obtained from analyzing the Maven Toys retail dataset using SQL.

---

## Inventory Analysis

### Inventory Value by Store

**Finding**

- The analysis identified the stores with the highest inventory investment.
- These stores represent the largest share of inventory capital.

**Business Impact**

- High inventory investment should be monitored to reduce carrying costs and optimize stock allocation.

---

### Top Products by Inventory Value

**Finding**

- A small group of products contributes significantly to the overall inventory value.

**Business Impact**

- These products require close inventory monitoring due to their higher financial impact.

---

### Product Category Analysis

**Finding**

- Inventory investment varies across product categories.

**Business Impact**

- Understanding category-wise investment helps prioritize purchasing and inventory planning.

---

## Sales Analysis

### Best Selling Products

**Finding**

- The analysis identified the products with the highest sales volume.

**Business Impact**

- High-performing products should be prioritized during inventory replenishment.

---

### Store Performance

**Finding**

- Sales performance differs across stores.

**Business Impact**

- High-performing stores may require more frequent inventory replenishment and resource allocation.

---

### Monthly Sales Trend

**Finding**

- Monthly sales analysis highlights periods of higher and lower customer demand.

**Business Impact**

- Seasonal demand patterns can support inventory planning and sales forecasting.

---

## Advanced Analysis

### Product Ranking

**Finding**

- Products were ranked using SQL window functions based on inventory value.

**Business Impact**

- Ranking enables quick identification of the most valuable inventory items.

---

### Inventory Distribution

**Finding**

- Inventory distribution is not uniform across stores or product categories.

**Business Impact**

- Balanced inventory allocation can improve stock availability while reducing excess inventory.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis demonstrates how SQL can be used to transform raw retail data into actionable business insights. The results can support inventory optimization, sales analysis, product performance evaluation, and data-driven business decision-making.
